Education

Drexel University

After high school, I enrolled at Drexel University in Philadelphia.

I chose Drexel for these reasons:

  • They had a new college of Information Science and Technology, which was not common in the mid-1990s
  • Coooperative Education provided the opportunity to gain real-world experience while in school
  • Proximity -- I wasn't interested on going away to school and the 25-minute drive made it easily commutable

Cooperative Education

The co-op program was excellent. Bachelor degree programs are typically 4 years, but the co-op program was a 5-year degree, with time built-in to gain work experience in order to start building a resume and get a head start over other graduates.

In the middle 3 years, students would work for 6 months and go to school for 6 months. (The semesters run on a quarterly schedule to make this work.)

It was a tremendous experience to go through the process of applying, interviewing, and then gaining real-world experience while still in school. It also provided a much better income than minimum wage work.

I ended up getting hired full-time after my third co-op cycle and my employer offered to pay my tuition as I finished up my senior year of classes at night.

ALN

During my third year as an undergraduate, I had the opportunity to work in my college's research lab, where the graduate students and a few professors were building what was among the very first online learning programs, dubbed the Asynchronous Learning Network (ALN) .

This program provided remote students the opportunity to earn a Master of Science in Information Systems completely online, circa 1995. This courses were built with Lotus Notes. The experience I gained here launched my career developing with the Notes platform.

Graduation

I was originally scheduled to graduate in 1999, but because I completed my senior year of classes at night while working full-time -- and also because of a car accident that necessitated two spinal surgeries -- I completed my studies and graduated in the spring of 2001, finishing at the top of my class with 4.0 GPA.
